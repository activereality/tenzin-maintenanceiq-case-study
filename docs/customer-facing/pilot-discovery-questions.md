# Pilot Discovery Questions

Use these questions to shape a practical Tenzin MaintenanceIQ pilot. The goal is to understand where maintenance knowledge lives today, which assets matter most, and what a useful pilot should validate.

## Facility Context

- What type of facility, process, or production environment would be included in the pilot?
- Which area, line, cell, or work center would be the best starting point?
- Who would sponsor the pilot from operations, maintenance, reliability, or engineering?

## Critical Assets

- Which assets cause the most downtime, troubleshooting effort, or documentation searches?
- Which assets have enough manuals, drawings, procedures, or prior notes to support a meaningful pilot?
- Are there safety-critical assets or procedures that should be excluded from early pilot scope?

## Existing Documentation

- Where do manuals, OEM documents, drawings, troubleshooting guides, and procedures live today?
- Which documents are approved for technicians to use during troubleshooting?
- Are documents organized by asset, facility, line, vendor, or shared-drive folder structure?
- Are there duplicate, outdated, or incomplete documents that should be cleaned up before upload?

## Information-Finding Pain Points

- What information is hardest to find during downtime or urgent troubleshooting?
- Which fault codes, alarms, symptoms, or procedures are searched for repeatedly?
- How much time do technicians typically spend looking through PDFs, binders, folders, or messages?

## Maintenance Workflow

- Who usually investigates equipment issues first: operators, technicians, reliability, controls, or engineering?
- How are troubleshooting notes, fixes, and follow-up actions captured today?
- Where does handoff happen between shifts, roles, or departments?

## CMMS / EAM Usage

- Which CMMS, EAM, work-order, or asset-management tools are already used?
- What information should stay in those systems rather than Tenzin MaintenanceIQ?
- Would the pilot need manual reference to existing work orders, asset names, or issue categories?

## Security And Data Concerns

- What document types are acceptable for a pilot, and what should be excluded?
- Are there concerns about vendor manuals, proprietary procedures, plant layouts, or controlled documents?
- Who should have access to pilot assets, documents, answers, citations, and issue context?
- What review or approval steps are needed before uploading documents?

## Pilot Scope And Success Metrics

- Which 3-10 assets would make a focused first pilot useful?
- Which document set should be uploaded first?
- What questions should the team expect Tenzin MaintenanceIQ to answer with cited sources?
- What would make the pilot worth expanding: faster document lookup, better citation traceability, technician usability, or improved issue capture?
